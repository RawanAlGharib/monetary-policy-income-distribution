# The Effectiveness of Conventional and Unconventional Monetary Policies During the COVID-19 Pandemic

## 📌 Project Overview
This project is a comprehensive literature review assessing the empirical effectiveness of central bank interventions during the COVID-19 pandemic. It explores how economies implemented expansionary monetary policies to mitigate crisis-induced financial disruptions, contrasting traditional approaches with unconventional tools (such as forward guidance and quantitative easing) utilized at the zero lower bound.

## 🎯 Objectives
* Outline the theoretical transmission channels through which monetary policies impact the broader economy.
* Survey and synthesize recent empirical literature on the effectiveness of monetary responses to the COVID-19 economic shock.
* Evaluate the comparative success and limitations of conventional versus unconventional monetary tools under conditions of extreme global uncertainty.

## 🛠️ Methodology & Core Skills
* **Research Type:** Literature Review / Theoretical Synthesis
* **Key Economic Concepts:** Macroeconomic Shocks, Monetary Policy Transmission Channels, Quantitative Easing, Zero Lower Bound, Crisis Management.
* **Core Skills Demonstrated:** Academic research, complex information synthesis, and professional economic writing.

## 📂 Repository Structure
* `docs/`: Contains the complete literature review paper detailing the theoretical transmission channels and the empirical survey of pandemic-era monetary policies.

## 📊 Key Takeaways from the Literature
1. **Conventional Policy Constraints:** Evidence suggests that the extreme uncertainty of the pandemic significantly lessened the effectiveness of the traditional transmission channels through which conventional monetary policy operates.
2. **Unconventional Policy Impact:** Unconventional tools successfully influenced the economy primarily through bond yields and stock prices; however, their success was highly contingent on the perceived commitment and intervention capacity of the central banks.
3. **Future Policy Implications:** The findings highlight the need to re-evaluate policy frameworks in highly uncertain environments to improve both the efficiency of conventional tools and the robust deployment of unconventional strategies.
